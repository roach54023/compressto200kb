Why Some Portals Require Images as Small as 20KB

Government portals that require 20KB images were typically built on older infrastructure with limited server storage and bandwidth. These systems store millions of applicant photos, so keeping each file small reduces storage costs significantly. While modern systems have moved to 100KB or 200KB limits, many legacy portals still enforce the 20KB limit.

How to Get the Best Quality at 20KB

20KB is a very small file size for a photo. Here's how to maximize quality:

Resize Before Compressing

If your portal requires a photo of 200×230 pixels, resize your image to exactly those dimensions before uploading to this tool. A 200×230px image at 20KB will look much better than a 1000×1000px image compressed to 20KB, because the compressor doesn't need to throw away as much detail.

Use a Plain White Background

Plain backgrounds compress extremely efficiently in JPEG format. A photo with a white background at 200×230px can easily fit in 20KB while keeping the face sharp.

Good Lighting Matters

Well-lit photos with even illumination compress better than dark or unevenly lit photos. According to Google's image compression research, images with less noise and more uniform areas achieve significantly better compression ratios.

Signature images are mostly white background with thin dark strokes — this is one of the most compressible types of image content for JPEG.
