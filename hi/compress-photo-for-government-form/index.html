How to Compress Photo for Government Form in 3 Steps

1

Select the correct target size for your exam

Check your exam notification for the exact photo size limit. Click the matching preset button above (20KB, 50KB, 100KB, or 200KB). For SSC and IBPS, use 50KB. For UPSC, NEET, JEE, use 100KB.

2

Upload your passport-size photo

Click Select Photo or drag and drop your JPG file. The photo should have a white or light background, be recent, and clearly show your face. The tool accepts JPG, PNG, and WEBP formats.

3

Download and verify before uploading

The compressed photo appears instantly with the exact file size shown. Click ⬇ Download to save it. Before uploading to the government portal, right-click the file → Properties to confirm the size is within the allowed range.

Important Tips for Government Form Photo Upload

Most Indian government exam portals share these common photo requirements. Make sure your photo meets all of them before compressing:

DimensionsTypically 3.5cm × 4.5cm (passport size)
ResolutionAt least 200 DPI for print quality

Why "File Too Large" Error Happens

Government portals show a "file too large" or "file size exceeds limit" error when your photo is above the maximum KB allowed. This is the most common upload error. Simply compress your photo to the required size using this tool and re-upload.

Why "File Too Small" Error Happens

Some portals also have a minimum file size (e.g., SSC requires at least 20KB). If your photo is too small, it may have been over-compressed. Use a higher target size — for example, if 20KB gives an error, try 30KB or 40KB.

Signature Compression

Most government forms also require a compressed signature image. The process is the same — scan or photograph your signature on white paper, then compress it to the required size using this tool. Typical signature size limits are 10–20KB for SSC/IBPS and 20–50KB for UPSC.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the photo size for SSC CGL form?
SSC CGL requires a photo between 20KB and 50KB in JPEG format. Use the 50KB preset on this tool to compress your photo to exactly 50KB or below. The photo dimensions should be 3.5cm × 4.5cm with a white background.
What is the photo size for UPSC form?
UPSC Civil Services (IAS/IPS) requires a photo between 40KB and 300KB. A 100KB JPEG is the recommended size — it meets the minimum, stays well under the maximum, and maintains excellent quality for identity verification.
What is the photo size for IBPS PO / Clerk form?
IBPS PO and Clerk require a photo between 20KB and 50KB in JPEG format, with dimensions of 200×230 pixels. Use the 50KB preset on this tool.
What is the photo size for NEET form?
NEET UG requires a photo between 10KB and 200KB. A 100KB JPEG is recommended for the best balance of quality and file size. The photo must have a white background and show a clear face.
What is the photo size for JEE Main form?
JEE Main requires a photo between 10KB and 200KB in JPEG format. Use the 100KB preset for the best quality within the allowed range.
Is it safe to compress my government form photo online?
Yes, completely safe. All compression happens inside your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API — your photo is never uploaded to any server, never stored, and never seen by anyone other than you. This is especially important for sensitive ID photos.
What format should the photo be for government forms?
Almost all Indian government portals require JPEG (.jpg) format. This tool automatically outputs JPEG files, which is the correct format for all government exam and application portals.
Can I compress both photo and signature with this tool?
Yes. You can compress your photo and signature separately. For the signature, scan it on white paper, then upload it to this tool and select the appropriate size (usually 10–20KB for SSC/IBPS, 20KB for UPSC).
